Reflections from Shereef's Fireside Chat

  • What's your take on the DBC/ EDA experience?

    • It's a different experience from other learning institutions as you are encouraged to collaborate with others in the group.

  • What are your impressions?

    • Apart from learning how to be a web developer, you are learning mindset skills that will help you get through problems that you may encounter, along with other skills such as time management and relaxation activities. I felt that Shereef was talking truthfully from his experience and he sounded sincere, as it came from the heart.

  • How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

    • From my previous study and work experience, I can hopefully take on board (embrace) these new skills that will set me up for the rest of my life. In order for me to engage in this culture I am going to have to pace myself through phase 0, master the basics and keep a good mindset to keep on track.

  • Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

    • My expectations have changed a bit, The phase 0 is a bit more practical (using the command line) than I anticipated. I didn't realize that sprint 1 has so much information on relaxation, mindset, meditation and time tracking.

  • Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

    • I am excited because I feel that it is going to teach me a lot. I am a little nervous at the moment, as I am catching up because i have had work commitments with bad hours. However I am enjoying the content of what I am learning and when I focus on it in this way it keeps me relaxed and on track for time management.
